Shortening the buyflow

Removing one step led to 3X conversion bump

We removed the registration confirmation step during the subscription purchase and activation flows as we realized (based on Allisonâ€™s observation) that it was unnecessary and confusing, thus impeding progress to checkout. Following the change, conversion increased ~3X.

Opportunity Discovery

Most of what we build is based on user insights, but this one came from our lead designer, who noticed a flaw in our purchase flow.

When a new user creates an account, they are taken to a confirmation page. The problem our designer noticed is that we were presenting this step to users who were attempting to subscribe, thus impeding their progress to checkout. To make matters worse, the confirmation page encouraged users to leave the flow and verify their account.

This was the oppostive of what we wanted from this segment of users, which was to make it as easy as possible to subscribe. When looking at our data, we realized many users were dropping out of the flow at this point.

What the team built

We realized the benefit of sending users directly to checkout far outweighed not showing them the confirmation step. So the change was simple - introduce logic that does not show the confirmation step to users who are attempting to subscribe.
